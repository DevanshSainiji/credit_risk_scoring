RBI Responsible Lending Guidelines & Financial Ethics
1. Fair Practice Code: Lenders must promote transparency, fairness, and sympathetic consideration for borrowers in genuine financial distress.
2. Debt-to-Income Limits: Borrowers with a debt ratio above 1.5 indicate severe financial stress. Further credit should be extended with extreme caution and heavily mitigated.
3. Repayment History: Recent delinquencies (late payments within 30-90 days) significantly elevate default risk. Over 3 late payments in a year generally disqualifies unsecured lending without manual review.
4. Age Discrimination: Refusal of credit solely based on age is unethical. However, lenders must ensure sufficient income streams for borrowers over retirement age.
5. Overleveraging: High revolving utilization (over 0.8) indicates potential dependency on credit. Borrowers displaying utilization above 1.0 are severely overleveraged and generally unsuitable for immediate new credit lines without a recovery plan.
6. Data Privacy and Algorithmic Bias: Automated decisions must be explainable. A loan cannot be rejected merely because an algorithm outputs 'high risk'—a structured reason based on financial metrics must be provided, avoiding arbitrary biases based on demographics.
7. Real Estate and Secured Lending: The presence of real estate loans indicates stability but must be balanced against liquid monthly income.
8. Missing Data: Handling missing fields gracefully is crucial. An application should not be automatically rejected for a single missing field unless it is legally required. Reasonable inferences and manual reviews should be recommended.
9. Extreme Recommendations: Lenders should avoid extreme recommendations without justification. Provide balanced options such as "Further Review Required" instead of outright rejection if confidence is low.
10. Low Confidence: If confidence in the Machine Learning model is low (e.g. probability near the threshold), human review MUST be mandated before adverse action.
